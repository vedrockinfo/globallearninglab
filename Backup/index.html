City Montessori School, Lucknow World’s Largest School Recipient UNESCO Prize for Peace Education

As the largest school in the world, City Montessori School (CMS), has always upheld the banner of quality education and has promoted an education based on principles of equity and excellence.

A K12 school in the city Lucknow, India, CMS started in 1959 with 5 children and the equivalent of 10 dollars in borrowed capital by Drs. Jagdish and Bharti Gandhi. The school was entered in the Guinness Book of World Records in 1999 and has since maintained its position as the world's largest school with the current enrollment of 61,000 children. CMS has promoted an education for peace and world unity. It received the UNESCO Prize for Peace Education in 2002. Let’s Step up the Act

Dear Changemaker

It is past half-time for SDG4. Progress is slow. The problems that were before the Covid with 52% of children unable to read, remains largely unchanged. Many countries have yeat to reach the Pre-Covid levels. A quality education is implausible without first addressing The problem of foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN).

A Crisis of Pedagogy

The much ignored fact is the crises of pedagogy. The crisis in learning is a crisis of pedagogy; both how we teach the foundational skills, and how quickly we teach them. Rote methods of teaching and learning are holding hostage enormous potential of the children, many of whom drop out of schools, and suffer indignation the rest of their lives.

How will the world convert its well-intentioned resolves into reality, like those made last September at the UN Transforming Education Summit in New York? What will the pathway to universal literacy be? When will foundational skills include climate change as their essential component now before COP 28? What new pedagogies and policies will it take to deliver foundational skills within weeks and months, and not years?

Accelerating Learning for AllPedagogy of the Future Today

ALfA, or Accelerating Learning for All, revolutionizes education with a child-led focus on foundational literacy and numeracy. Given the stark fact that 2 out of 3 children under ten lack basic skills, ALfA seeks to correct educational failures. This innovative pedagogy ensures rapid literacy and numeracy acquisition in just 45 days, prioritizing effectiveness, joy, and well-being.

Going beyond conventional learning, ALfA strives to offer holistic education for every child. Envisioning a collaborative and conflict-free future, ALfA equips children with skills to shape a climate conscious peaceful world free from poverty—a crucial approach for a brighter global reality.

ALfA 4C Skills + 4C Traits

ALfA seamlessly integrates the 4C Skills leading to 4C Traits of a twenty-first century learner. These 8Cs cannot be taught from a textbook, but rather acquired while working with each other in the everyday context of a classroom. Pair setting creates a safe environment in which learners feel free to share and express

WHY DISRUPT?

There is nothing to lose. The traditional education system is failing the children:

  • 70% of 10-year-olds in low and middle income countries (LMICs) can’t read
  • There are 800 million adults around the world who lack the skill to read, write and do basic arithmetic.

WHAT KIND OF CHANGE?

Pedagogy is as important as actions to address the crises. We need radical educational reform:

  • A switch to swift, effective programs with a low climate footprint
  • An education beyond academic skills that helps create a more humane world

EVIDENCE OF IMPACT?

A recent book edited by Prof. Fernando Reimurs of Harvard Graduate School of Education found that ALfA (Accelerating Learning for All) implementation in government schools of Shamli in 40 days had an effect size of 0.23 for Grade 3 and 0.89 for Grade 5. Children doubled their Reading Fluency

THE WAY FORWARD?

The ALfA program has taken off in just one year to diverse contexts around the world with numerous government, non-government organizations, and private schools bringing fast-track literacy to all.

Global Ed Innovator Fellowships & Awards

Fellowships and Awards are our way of saying: “Thank you for being a part of the change process so urgently needed in education.” Fellowships are for promising ideas for research and innovations-in-action. Awards are for programs which are already able to demonstrate their impact with evidence.

INTERNATIONAL JURY

An international jury will make the selections based on transparent criteria to award between 100 to 200 Fellowships this year depending upon the quality of the submissions.

FELLOWSHIPS

All GETI Fellows are expected to carry out their ideas for change post selection, and to gather evidence of their implementation on a regular basis in an e-portfolio. Evidence includes photos, videos, slide presentations, surveys, pre-and post-intervention data and its analysis (quantitative, anecdotal and qualitative) as in action research.

All GETI Fellows with evidence will automatically qualify for the Global Ed Innovator Awards.
